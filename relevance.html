Fairchild Semiconductor

Despite Fairchild Semiconductor no longer being a functional company, after being taken over by ON Semiconductor in 2016,
many of the inventions and developments made whilst Moore was there are still relevant today.
For example, when Moore worked on the production of transistors, he decided to use aluminium as the metal used for the contacts, which is still the metal of choice for silicon devices today. The planar process was also developed whilst Moore was at Fairchild, where individual components of a transistor are built, and then those transistors are put together. This is still in use today and is the primary process by which silicon integrated circuit chips are built.

Moore's Law Today

Being first theorised in 1965, and then revised 10 years later in 1975, so far, Moore's law has held true despite numerous technological innovations during that time. However, we are unlikely to ever know whether it would hold true in a world where it was not predicted, as scientists may have been developing CPUs so Moore's law was true, or it could be that he made an amazingly correct prediction which has held true for the past 46 years.

Relevance of Intel Today

Intel first started with a goal to enter the semiconductor memory market, which was predicted to replace magnetic-core memory. In 1971, they released their first microprocessor. In the early 1980s, Intel's business was dominated by dynamic random access chips, however, due to growing competition from Japanese companies, Intel pivoted to primarily making microprocessors, which it still does today. Present day, Intel has lost much of its market share in the high-end CPU market, due to AMD's resurgence. Apple has also moved away from Intel's processors. Despite these recent drawbacks, Intel's contribution to personal computers cannot be overstated. They created the first commercially available microprocessor, as well as having their microprocessors being used by IBM for their personal computers. Intel has been at the forefront of the technological hardware market for many years and it is hard to see that come to an end.
